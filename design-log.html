Design Log

Week 1

Monday 11th November - We learnt what was going to be covered across the five weeks of this module. For web design specifically we were introduced to GitHub and sorted out our accounts. We also had a look at what web design is, and the key terms such as ‘HTML’ and ‘CSS’. We also chose our themes for our website and downloaded them to our laptops. We learnt this as it is hugely significant in understanding the basics of web design.  For Photography we began to look at key elements to consider when taking photos such as shutter speed and aperture. We also had a brief practice at taking photos using plastic figures which was a first chance to develop basic photography skills. We learnt this to be able to develop skills in the classroom so that it will be easier to develop skills out in the city center. The basic web design session will be useful continuously across the module, this was useful for developing an understanding which could then be used as a framework for producing the website. The theme will also be carried forward into the whole of the module. Therefore, these skills will be used in the portfolio.  For photography it was useful to learn the basics with so much help as this will allow us to take good images for our website. Also, video was mentioned when talking about camera work which will consequently be beneficial across different elements of the portfolio.  

Tuesday 12th November - For web design we learnt how to use GitHub to push up elements of the website to make the changes live. We also began to look at using atom to design our website. This was important for ensuring that we can publish changes to a live website. For photography we looked at elements such as composition so that we will be able to make the most interesting portfolio. This is important as it made us notice that how you stage a photo can determine its impact.  Using GitHub will be useful in the whole development of the portfolio as it allows the website to be live. Atom skills are essential to changing the website so this will be significant in the module.  For photography, this will be implemented by using the skills to get the best possible pictures for my portfolio. 

Wednesday 13th November - For Digital Wednesday we looked at what elements of a website make it effective. We looked at Dieter Rams’ 10 principles of good design. We also found examples of websites that demonstrated the key principles. We did this as it was useful to understand what aspects of web design are the most important as this can then be used throughout this module to ensure that my website is as effective as possible.  I will consider all the elements of website design to ensure that my portfolio/website is as effective as possible to best present my work.  

Thursday 14th November - In relation to web design, at this point we learnt how to set up different linked pages within the website. We did this as we would be able to display different aspects of the module clearly in separate pages of the website including; photography, audio, a design log, a video interview and a contacts page. We also had a guest speaker come in this day. He was a manager in marketing for Salford F.C., his talk was useful for showing how important branding and use of websites are for promoting yourself or a company as well as possible. This was to help us by hearing from someone with huge amounts of experience. The different links will be implemented in all aspects of the portfolio as they separate out the different aspects of the module allowing it to be as effective as possible. The discussion with the guest speaker will be useful as we have seen the benefits of good web design and camera skills which will motivate us to use it in our portfolio.  

Friday 15th November - In web design we focused on typography. We learnt the different categories of fonts including sans serif to understand the significance of fonts within design. We then learnt how to change fonts through google fonts. This was then used to change our website. We learnt this as it was important to choose appropriate fonts to help the website look professional.  Due to a TTC talk there was not a large amount of time spent on photography.  The font is important for the portfolio as a good choice of fonts can portray a sense of professionalism whilst a bad choice of fonts could hinder its impact. Therefore, the lessons in typography will be implemented in the portfolio.
